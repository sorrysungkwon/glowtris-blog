So I built a Tetris game called [Glowtris](https://glowtris.com). Am I a game developer? Hell no. I'm a designer. I can't code. Never could.

But I can talk. And Claude can code. So I literally just... talked at Claude for like a month and it built the whole thing. Game, UI, brand identity, blog—all of it.

## This blog is basically my live debugging log

Ngl, most of this is gonna be me going "oh shit, this is broken, here's why" and then "wait, actually the fix is kinda cool." I'm not some expert explaining best practices. I'm just a guy who discovered that setTimeout-based game loops are a nightmare, that PixiJS on Safari is a special kind of hell, and that redis buckets are pretty clever for leaderboards.

## What's actually in here

**Real code problems**  
Not tutorials. Just "I was trying to do X, it completely failed, and here's what I learned."

**Random engineering discoveries**  
Fixed timesteps. Canvas vs WebGL. Why my Vercel builds kept timing out. The stuff nobody blogs about because it's "obvious" but it's not.

**Design shit that worked**  
Emoji-first UI. Neon gradients that don't suck. Keeping a retro game feeling modern. (Spoiler: it's hard.)

**Honest mistakes**  
I'll tell you when I was dumb. When I over-engineered something. When I should have just used a simpler solution.

기본적으로 여기는 내 실시간 디버깅 로그다.

전문가도 아니고, 뭘 모르는 디자이너가 AI로 게임을 만들면서 뭐가 일어났는지 기록하는 거다. 테트리스 깬 이야기, 래더보드 고쳤는데 이게 실제로 진짜 쓸 만한 트릭였던 이야기, Vercel에서 빌드가 자꾸 터지는 이야기 이런 거다.

정보를 구하는 거면 이 블로그가 도움이 될 거고, 누군가 똑같은 문제를 겪고 있으면 "어? 이 사람도 같은 거 겪었네?" 하고 웃을 수 있을 거다.

그럼 시작하자.
