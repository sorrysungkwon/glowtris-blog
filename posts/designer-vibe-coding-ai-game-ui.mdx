I am a designer with 7 years of experience. I've hustled through New York and Seoul, perfectly mastering color theory, whitespace, and typography along the way.

But I am absolutely hopeless at coding. I still have no idea why `npm install` blows up randomly.

Yet, without writing a single line of code, I built an entire browser-based Tetris game, **[Glowtris](www.glowtris.com)**, and this very blog just by **talking aggressively** to Claude.

Vibe coding is absolutely not a magic wand that does everything for you. But it did completely flip my workflow:

1. **Describe the picture in my head** → 2. **AI spits something out** → 3. **I point and say "That's wrong"** → 4. **AI fixes it**

The AI does the physical labor, but the **judgment of what is right or wrong belongs entirely to the human.** Especially for UI/UX, those 7 years I spent grinding in the design industry became my absolute superpower. It's a whole different world from the days when I couldn't even voice an opinion because I didn't know how to code. It feels like having a blisteringly fast senior developer locked in a room as your partner... err, hostage.

## The Glowtris Color System: How to make AI listen

The core concept for Glowtris was simple: **"It must look like a neon arcade game, but shouldn't burn your retinas after 20 minutes."**

My first prompt was this:
> "Make the background a very dark navy, almost black. The neon glow colors for the blocks should be cyan, magenta, yellow, and green. The UI should look like a dark interface from a sci-fi movie — clean, minimal, and glowing."

Claude spat out a CSS file with a `#000000` background and some neon colors. But there was a massive problem: **Pure black murders the glow effect.** Light looks infinitely better bleeding into a deep, dark blue-black rather than a void. So, I instantly threw a fit.

> "The background is way too flat. It needs to be a deep, desaturated navy — something like `#04041e`. The Tetris blocks must look like they are physically emitting light, damn it."

The result was a resounding success. That `#04041e` value I randomly spat out became the actual production background color.

## Typography System: My professor was right

For the logo, I used `Orbitron`, a heavy, geometric sci-fi typeface. You can smell the "space game" vibes from 100 meters away.

But if you use that for body text, your readability goes straight to the garbage bin. So, I demanded the AI to completely separate the font system.

> "Header and logo: `Orbitron`. Body and UI: `Pretendard` for Korean, `system-ui` for English. Monospace elements like code blocks: `JetBrains Mono`."

Splitting fonts into Display / Body / Mono is the exact standard pattern my UI professor, Jae-Hwi Na, drilled into my skull in college. It turns out, AI perfectly understands and executes the well-paved roads laid down by our seniors.

## 4 Things You Can NEVER Fix With Prompts

Is AI an omnipotent god? Nope. I stared at my monitor until my eyeballs fell out, always having to **manually** fix these 4 things:

1. **Spacing**: The CSS spacing AI spits out is always either claustrophobically tight or idiotically wide. I constantly had to reduce logo kerning, expand section margins, and tweak card padding using my own visual intuition.
2. **Typography Scale**: AI gives you a mathematically perfect scale, but it looks flat and boring to the human eye. I always forcibly blew up the heading sizes and shrank the body text to maximize contrast.
3. **Color Interactions**: AI is decent at picking single colors, but when multiple colors interact (like inverting active button text in dark mode), it drops the ball almost every single time.
4. **Micro-interactions**: AI hover animations are either too timid or obnoxiously bouncy. I ruthlessly enforced `0.15s ease` with fast response times to make the hovers feel confident.

## Conclusion & Practical Tips

At the end of the day, every final product must be dripping with **your own subjective opinions.**

Claude is insanely fast. But if you don't know what you want, it will just hand you a beautifully functioning piece of garbage. Your success or failure rests entirely on the prompts that come out of **your mouth.**

Are you a designer sitting on a goldmine of ideas just because you don't know how to code? Just start talking to an AI. You can absolutely do this!
